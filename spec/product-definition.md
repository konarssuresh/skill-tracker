# Product Definition: Skills Learning Tracker

## What

Skills Learning Tracker is a personal practice journal where users log sessions, track progress across any skill they're learning, and visualize their consistency through streaks, total hours, and heatmap calendars. "Strava for learning" — a deliberate practice companion that makes the invisible work of skill-building visible and motivating.

## Who

Learners who want a simple, beautiful way to track their practice habits — whether they're picking up guitar, studying Spanish, learning TypeScript, improving their UI design skills, or mastering a new recipe.

**User profiles:**

- **The habit builder** who practices guitar 30 minutes a day and wants to see their streak grow. Wants visual proof of consistency — streaks, heatmaps, and total hours that reward showing up.
- **The multi-skill juggler** who's learning Spanish, TypeScript, and cooking simultaneously. Wants a single dashboard that shows progress across all their skills without switching between apps.
- **The goal-driven learner** who sets a target — "Log 200 hours of AI engineering this year" — and wants to track their pace toward it. Wants to know if they're ahead or behind, and how much time they need per week to stay on track.
- **The reflective practitioner** who journals after each session — what they worked on, what clicked, what needs more attention. Wants a log they can look back on to see growth over weeks and months.

## Why

Learning a skill is a long game. Progress is slow, invisible, and easy to underestimate. Most people quit not because they aren't improving, but because they can't see their improvement. The gap between "I've been practicing" and "I can see how much I've practiced" is where motivation dies.

Existing tools don't serve this well. Habit trackers are too generic — they track "did you do it?" but not "for how long?" or "what did you work on?" Time trackers are built for billing, not for personal growth. And journaling apps lack the visual feedback that makes practice feel rewarding.

Skills Learning Tracker sits in the sweet spot: structured enough to capture meaningful data (duration, notes, skill category), simple enough to log a session in under 30 seconds, and visual enough to make every practice session feel like it counts.

## Core Value Proposition

**"See every hour you've invested in becoming better."**

## What Makes This a Strong Product Challenge

- **Immediately visual and compelling** — Progress rings, streak calendars, and heatmaps make every screen rich with data visualization. The guest experience is instantly engaging because the numbers and visuals tell a story without explanation.
- **Self-contained data model** — No external API needed. Skills, sessions, and users form a clean 2-3 table schema that's approachable for intermediate developers while still requiring thoughtful design decisions.
- **Rich design surface** — A bento-style dashboard, progress rings, heatmap calendars, session logs, and skill cards create diverse design opportunities across very different component types.
- **Universal appeal** — Everyone is learning something. A skill tracker resonates with developers (who are always learning), hiring managers (who value growth mindset), and the broader community.
- **Natural motivation loop** — Streaks, hours, and visual progress create a compelling "just one more session" dynamic that makes the product feel genuinely useful, not just a demo.
